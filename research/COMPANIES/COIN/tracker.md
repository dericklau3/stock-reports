## Investment view

### Why not outright bullish now

- Q1 transaction revenue and Trading Volume dropped sharply.
- GAAP net loss was **-$394M**.
- Current valuation still needs cycle recovery and new products to work.
- Revenue/profit are highly sensitive to crypto prices, volatility and trading volume.
- Regulatory, security and stablecoin economics risks remain material.

### Why keep on watchlist

- Coinbase has a strong regulated brand and large AOP.
- S&S already reached **44% of net revenue** in Q1.
- Stablecoin revenue remains large and strategically important.
- Deribit acquisition expands derivatives/options exposure.
- Base, Coinbase One, prediction markets, DEX integrations and Everything Exchange create upside optionality.
- Balance sheet is strong with net cash.

## Key thesis drivers

1. Crypto spot and derivatives trading volume.
2. Retail MTUs and consumer transaction revenue take rate.
3. S&S durability, especially stablecoin revenue.
4. USDC market cap and USDC held in Coinbase products.
5. Deribit integration and crypto options/perps share.
6. Base chain usage, DEX trading and developer adoption.
7. Expense discipline after AI-native restructuring.
8. SBC dilution vs. repurchase offset.
9. Regulatory clarity around staking, stablecoins, derivatives, token listings and yield products.
10. Security and customer trust.

## Key risks

- Crypto market volume/volatility remains weak.
- Stablecoin revenue declines due to rates, USDC share, balances or revenue-share economics.
- Regulatory actions restrict staking, derivatives, stablecoins, token listings or yield products.
- New cybersecurity/data/customer trust event.
- Deribit goodwill/intangibles impairment or weak integration.
- Fee compression from CEX/DEX/Robinhood/traditional brokers.
- SBC/share-count dilution.
- Mission drift from Everything Exchange expansion.
- CLO transition disrupts regulatory/legal execution.
