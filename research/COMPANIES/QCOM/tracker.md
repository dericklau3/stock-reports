## Current research stance

- **View**: Constructive but watchful（建设性，但等待 Q3/Q4 手机周期和多元化验证）
- **Confidence**: B
- **Core conclusion**: QCOM is a high-quality cash-flow semiconductor/IP platform trading around ~15x FCF and ~18x forward PE, with real shareholder returns. Current valuation is reasonable, but the stock still depends on handset stabilization and the credibility of automotive/IoT/data-center diversification.
- **Best use of this memo**: Track Q3 FY2026 earnings on 2026-07-29 and whether management's handset-bottom call is confirmed.

## Key thesis

Qualcomm combines two profit engines: QCT semiconductor platforms and QTL high-margin patent licensing. The market discounts QCOM because handset revenue remains large and exposed to Apple/customer vertical integration, China/Huawei and smartphone cycles. The upside comes from proving that automotive, IoT, edge AI and data-center custom silicon can become durable second growth curves while FCF and buybacks continue.

## Risks to track

1. Handset cycle remains weaker for longer.
2. Apple / major customer vertical integration reduces QCOM content.
3. China/Huawei/geopolitical/export-control pressure.
4. QTL licensing model faces customer, legal or regulatory pressure.
5. Automotive/IoT/data-center diversification fails to offset handset maturity.
6. Alphawave/Modular integration causes dilution, amortization or impairment without revenue contribution.
7. Inventory and memory-supply constraints pressure QCT margins.

## Red-line conditions

- Q3/Q4 handset revenue does not stabilize despite management's bottoming commentary.
- QTL revenue or EBT margin materially deteriorates.
- QCT Automotive growth sharply slows without explanation.
- Data-center custom silicon slips without new timeline or quantitative evidence.
- Share count stops declining because SBC/acquisition issuance overwhelms buybacks.
- China/Apple exposure worsens enough to reduce medium-term EPS power.

## What would change the view

- **Upgrade to Positive**: Q3 meets/beat guide, handset bottom is confirmed, Q4 outlook improves, Automotive/IoT continue double-digit growth, and data-center initial shipments become quantifiable while FCF remains strong.
- **Downgrade to Neutral/Negative**: Q3 miss or weak Q4 guide, handset bottom fails, QTL weakens, inventory rises materially, or acquisitions dilute shareholders without evidence of strategic payoff.
